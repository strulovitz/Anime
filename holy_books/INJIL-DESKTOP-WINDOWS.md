ROLE — THE STUDIO. Three crafts live here:
1. EDITING: Adobe Premiere 23 (installed by Nir) — the assembly hall
   where backgrounds, characters, video clips, voices, music, songs and
   subtitles become finished episodes. OBS Studio for real-camera and
   screen recording. AviDemux for hard-coding subtitles (Nir knows how).
2. MUSIC: Ableton Live 12 (DAW) + Omnisphere 2 by Spectrasonics (software
   synthesizer with a vast instrument library) — both owned by Nir. An
   MCP bridge connects an AI assistant to Ableton, so Nir speaks plain
   English ("make the strings warmer", "raise the tempo") and the AI
   drives the DAW. The exact MCP server is chosen at install time from a
   live Google AI search — the state of the art moves monthly and this
   book must not freeze a stale choice; the decision and its date are
   then written HERE.
3. VR: Pianogram (Meta store) — loads any MIDI file and teaches it at
   any speed, no sheet-music reading needed. Future duty: reviewing
   rendered space scenes in VR to judge their scale and depth.

FILE TRANSFER LAW (decided 2026-07-23): Syncthing syncs media folders

small files (hard 100 MB per-file limit — never media). The finished

## PART 2 — THE MUSIC DOCTRINE (source: Nir + Fable, 2026-07-23)

THE LEITMOTIF DOCTRINE. The soundtrack model is Legend of the Galactic
Heroes: hard science fiction scored with classical music. Every main
character receives a romantic-era theme (a leitmotif) that follows them
through the whole series:
- MADIE = the solo violin of Scheherazade (Rimsky-Korsakov). Not
  arbitrary: Scheherazade IS a storyteller telling tales of voyages, and
  Alpha Babes is a voyage told episode by episode. The storyteller's
  violin IS Madie's voice.
- Other crew members: themes from Grieg (Peer Gynt), Tchaikovsky
  (Nutcracker, Swan Lake) and other romantic-era composers, assigned as
  their personalities emerge, one per girl, written here when chosen.
This is copyright-safe by construction: the COMPOSITIONS are public
domain, and the PERFORMANCES are Nir's own. No commercial recording is
ever used in the anime.

THE PIANO PIPELINE (how Nir, who does not read sheet music, performs
classical music — every step verified against his own hardware):
1. Obtain a public-domain MIDI of the piece (piano arrangement).
2. Load it into Pianogram in VR; slow it down as far as needed; practice
   until the hands know it.
3. Play it on the Yamaha P-515; the performance is captured as MIDI over
   USB into Ableton Live.
4. In Ableton, raise the tempo: MIDI speeds up LOSSLESSLY — no pitch
   change, no distortion. A piece practiced at quarter speed plays back
   perfectly at full speed. This is the answer to "play slow, hear it
   normal".
5. Record in passes, one musical role at a time: one pass as the solo
   violin line, one pass as the harp chords, one pass as the low strings
   — each pass gets its own Omnisphere instrument (chosen from Smart
   Pianist during practice, from Omnisphere in production). Layered
   together, they become an orchestra played by one man.
6. Export stems; carry to Premiere; marry to picture.

THE HUMAN HANDS SCENE (canon — must never be cut). OBS records the real
Nir at the real piano, playing slowly, for Madie. This footage goes
AFTER THE CREDITS of Episode 1. The entire series is AI-made — and then
the last thing Madie sees is human hands, playing slowly, for her. The
whole vibe-anime philosophy in one shot: the machines draw the dream;
the human provides the love.
