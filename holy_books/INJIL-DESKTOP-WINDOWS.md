THE PIANO PIPELINE (how Nir, who reads zero sheet music and knows zero
theory, performs classical music — the founding technique of the
vibe-anime home musician):
1. Obtain a public-domain MIDI of the piece (piano arrangement).
2. Load it into Pianogram in VR (Meta store); slow it down as far as
   needed; practice until the hands know it. Already proven: Nir has
   run a piano arrangement of Scheherazade in Pianogram.
3. Play it on the P-515; capture the performance as MIDI over USB into
   Ableton Live. Choose practice instruments from Smart Pianist.
4. In Ableton, raise the tempo: MIDI speeds up LOSSLESSLY — no pitch
   change. A piece practiced at quarter speed plays back perfectly at
   full speed. Fixes of wrong notes are simple MIDI edits.
5. Record in passes, one musical role at a time: one pass the solo
   violin line, one pass the harp chords, one pass the low strings —
   each pass gets its own Omnisphere rack (see the .adg ritual in
   ZABUR). Layered together: an orchestra played by one man.
6. Export stems; into Premiere; marry to picture. When wanted, send the
   raw MIDI via Syncthing to the Desk (ZABUR) for AI cleanup,
   quantizing, and orchestration — the two studios pass work back and
   forth like bandmates.
