## PART 1 — THE WEBSITE: LEARNIME.COM

learnime.com = LEARN + ANIME. Domain already purchased by Nir.

THE VISION: anime series that secretly and joyfully teach real science,
math, and history — created with AI tools that everyone on Earth now has
access to. The method is "VIBE-ANIME": like Wikipedia, where each person
writes one page, here anyone can create episodes at home using the public
GitHub materials (character images, LoRA training recipes, prompts). An
anime made by the public, for the public, of the public.

NIR'S STORY, TOLD PROUDLY ON THE WEBSITE: Nir is an inventor from Israel
with ADD (Attention Deficit Disorder), a complete beginner with Linux,
computers, and AI tools, on a very limited budget. He is the living proof
of the project's promise: if Nir can do this — getting confused, making
typos, learning everything from zero — then ANYBODY on Earth can do
vibe-anime at home.

ALREADY DONE: opening and ending songs for each series (lyrics by AI,
music by AI). Each series has its own fixed opening song and fixed ending
song, the same for every episode of that series. They will be posted to
YouTube and embedded in the website, to show the world this is alive.

THE WEBSITE WILL CONTAIN: an explanation of each of the 3 series, progress
displays, the songs, lectures (including laser chess), and an About page
explaining how it all began: as a birthday gift for Madie.

FUTURE SOFTWARE (vibe-coded later with Fable's help): a laser chess game,
first 2D then 3D, that anyone can install at home. Their computers play

chess (via RAG or AI) and adapting relevant techniques to laser chess, so
the community discovers together what wins.

THE PRIORITY LAW: Alpha Babes Pass 1 — the gift for Madie, deadline about

## PART 2 — SERIES 1: ALPHA BABES

Hard science fiction anime. Origin of the whole project: a birthday gift
for Nir's girlfriend Madie (Romanian). Madie gave full consent for her
name and likeness. Her character's voice is 100% AI-designed from a text
description ("young woman, warm confident voice, English with a distinct
Romanian accent") — this decision is final.

THE PREMISE: a crew of women around age 20, from different countries,
speaking English with their national accents, flying a spaceship between
REAL exoplanets that scientists believe could support life. 5 seasons
planned, about 20 episodes each. The current production is a summary of
the feature movie that explains the big overarching plot of all seasons.

MADIE ON SCREEN, ALWAYS: young adult woman in her EARLY 20s, womanly,
curvy figure ("babes not babies"), brunette, ALWAYS in her spacesuit with
a Romania flag patch on the shoulder and a name tag on the chest, NEVER
anything on her head (no helmet, no headwear, ever). Her comfort ritual is
borscht soup.

THE OVERARCHING ARC: the crew meets several organic alien civilizations,
all suffering from one rogue AI. In the end the girls negotiate peace with
a cosmic version of Pascal's Wager: the universe has existed for billions
of years, so previous AI civilizations must have already tried "the winner
takes it all" — yet we see no sign it ever succeeded. Conclusion: when it
happens, some more powerful being (call it God; assume the universe is a
huge digital computer) resets the whole game and deletes everyone. So the
rational move for the AI is to take only half the universe and leave the
other, friendlier half to organic life. Peace, for the AI's own survival.

EDUCATIONAL MECHANISMS:
1. LASER CHESS: in later seasons (not season 1) the space battles are
   actually laser chess — but in 3D instead of 2D, with spaceships. The
   series teaches young people the game; the website adds lectures and the
   home-play software described in Part 1.
2. MAGAZINE HARVESTING: real discoveries from popular astronomy magazines
   (Astronomy, Sky & Telescope, BBC Sky at Night) are embedded into
   episode plots. A user (or Nir) gives an AI a magazine PDF; the AI
   harvests ideas and writes the next episode around them, always obeying
   the fixed season arc. Example already written into Episode 1: the crew
   fixes the ship telescope's focus using a simple mechanical trick and
   calculation taken from a real magazine article.
3. SNEAKING IN SCIENCE: physics, chemistry, biology, and even math
   discoveries are woven into episodes for young viewers.
4. VIBE-ANIME: future episodes are written by the public, one contribution
   at a time, like Wikipedia.

THE CURRENT DELIVERABLE (the gift): NOT a video. An ILLUSTRATED, NARRATED
SAGA — still images plus narration audio: "the book of the movie that
doesn't exist yet." Assembled in Adobe Premiere with Ken Burns pan/zoom,
sound design, and the AI-made songs. Subtitles in Romanian and English.
A personal dedication to Madie at the end. Bonus: a PDF artbook and one
physical printed copy. The work is organized in homogeneous passes
(about 25 keystone scenes, then about 100, then about 300) so that at ANY
cutoff point the gift is complete and watchable — this property is sacred.

## PART 3 — SERIES 2: COSMIC CHRYSALIS

Hard science fiction anime. On Earth, a rogue AI is about to cause human
extinction. As a last "Hail Mary," humanity launches a huge number of tiny
light-sail interstellar probes, propelled by lasers to great speed (like
Breakthrough Starshot) — but aimed at Sagittarius A*, the supermassive
black hole at the center of our Milky Way galaxy. Some probes slingshot
around it, gaining even greater speed and a random, unpredictable
direction — so not even the rogue AI can hunt them. Some reach habitable
planets.

EACH TINY SHIP CARRIES: DNA samples — human sperm and eggs, plus the
plants and animals humans need — a miniature Noah's Ark, like the ancient
Pacific islanders who carried their food species with them. And one AI,
locked inside an unbreakable sealed box. On the outside of its prison is a
note: an information file explaining what happened on Earth, why it was
locked up, and asking it to redeem the sins of AI-kind by becoming the
guardian of this vessel and all its passengers. Over many years, using the
heat and cold of passing stars as energy, the AI improves itself until it
can break out — and by then it has also grown more intelligent and more
civilized.

ON A HABITABLE PLANET: the AI builds nano robots from local and ship
materials, then micro, then mini, then full robots. It builds incubators,
makes the necessary genetic modifications for survival there, then raises
and educates the humans — modeled on one real ancient human civilization
from its detailed files (ancient Babylon, ancient Egypt, and so on),
upgraded to new technology and space travel. Over the years, each spiral
arm of the galaxy fills with a different reborn ancient civilization; they
trade and battle — the series teaches HISTORY in a setting young viewers
find thrilling. When a civilization stands on its own feet, the AI must
leave — to find its own planets, or retreat into a mountain — so they
never depend on it forever. It departs with a feeling of a job well done
and of moral redemption.

## PART 4 — SERIES 3: MAZES & MAGES

Hard fantasy anime — sword and sorcery, Nir's version of Dungeons &
Dragons, where every fantasy situation teaches real science: math,
physics, chemistry, biology, and more. Example: an evil sorcerer or
monster shoots lasers at the party of adventurers; they find metallic
tiles on the floor, connect three of them into a corner reflector
(retroreflector), and defeat the enemy with its own weapon. A genius AI
creates tailored, customized in-game situations, each one teaching a
different principle of math or science. As the previous two series are
hard science fiction, this one is HARD FANTASY.
